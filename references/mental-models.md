# Mental Models

Quick reference for cognitive frameworks worth keeping front of mind.

---

## Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)
80% of outcomes come from 20% of causes. Focus ruthlessly on the inputs that matter.
[Pareto Principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pareto_principle)

## Parkinson's Law
Work expands to fill the time available. Set tighter deadlines deliberately.
[Parkinson's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parkinson%27s_law)

## Planning Fallacy
We consistently underestimate time, cost, and risk on future tasks — even when we know past projects ran over. Use reference class forecasting.
[Planning Fallacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planning_fallacy)

## Dunning-Kruger Effect
Low competence leads to overconfidence; high competence leads to underestimating your own ability relative to others.
[Dunning-Kruger Effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dunning%E2%80%93Kruger_effect)

## Occam's Razor
Among competing explanations, prefer the one with the fewest assumptions.
[Occam's Razor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occam%27s_razor)

## Hanlon's Razor
Never attribute to malice what can be adequately explained by stupidity (or ignorance, or incompetence).
[Hanlon's Razor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanlon%27s_razor)

## Inversion
Instead of asking how to achieve a goal, ask what would guarantee failure — then avoid those things.
[Inversion](https://fs.blog/inversion/)

## Second-Order Thinking
Ask "and then what?" Consider downstream consequences, not just immediate effects.
[Second-Order Thinking](https://fs.blog/second-order-thinking/)

## First Principles Thinking
Break a problem down to its fundamental truths and reason up from there, rather than by analogy.
[First Principles Thinking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_principle)

## Compound Effect
Small, consistent actions compound dramatically over time. Works for habits, skills, debt, and savings alike.
[Compound Effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compound_interest)

## Always Do Extra
In leftover time, don't do *More* (more of the same work) — do *Extra* (self-directed tangential learning). More helps the project; Extra builds you. The distinction is the secret to longevity in a technical career.
[Always Do Extra](https://www.bennorthrop.com/Essays/2021/always-do-extra.php)

## Decision Types (Bezos / One-Way vs Two-Way Doors)
Type 1 decisions are one-way doors — irreversible, high consequence, decide slowly. Type 2 decisions are two-way doors — reversible, decide quickly with a bias toward action. Conflating the two is where orgs get either reckless or paralyzed.
[Decision Types](https://www.theuncertaintyproject.org/tools/decision-types)

## Doing Things Right vs. Doing The Right Things
Drucker's distinction: *efficiency* is doing things right; *effectiveness* is doing the right things. You can be perfectly efficient at work that never should have been done. Effectiveness comes first.
[Doing the Right Things](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Drucker)

## Conway's Law
Organizations design systems that mirror their own communication structure. If you want a different architecture, change the team structure first.
[Conway's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conway%27s_law)

## Circle of Competence
Know what you know — and more importantly, know what you don't. Operating outside your circle of competence without acknowledging it is how smart people make catastrophic mistakes.
[Circle of Competence](https://fs.blog/circle-of-competence/)
